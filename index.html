Common questions

Nonprofit filing FAQs

Do all nonprofits file the same Form 990?

No. Depending primarily on gross receipts and assets, an organization may file Form 990-N, Form 990-EZ, or the full Form 990. Certain organizations and activities have different rules. We confirm the likely form based on your facts.

Is California Form 199 the same as the federal Form 990?

No. Form 199 is a California Franchise Tax Board information return. Some smaller organizations may qualify to submit Form 199N instead. It is separate from the federal filing.

What is the RRF-1?

Form RRF-1 is the annual registration renewal used by charitable organizations registered with the California Attorney General's Registry of Charities and Fundraisers. Required supporting filings vary by organization.

When are nonprofit returns due?

Form 990 series returns are generally due on the 15th day of the fifth month after the fiscal year ends. California filing deadlines and extension rules should be confirmed for your organization. Calendar-year organizations commonly face a May 15 deadline.

Can you help if our bookkeeping is not ready?

Yes. We can assess the books, define the cleanup needed, and quote that work separately before preparing the returns.

Do you also provide ongoing accounting support?

Yes. Services can include QuickBooks setup and cleanup, monthly close, restricted-fund and grant tracking, financial statements, board reporting, and controller-level support.
