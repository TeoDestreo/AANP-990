Common questions

Nonprofit filing FAQs

Do all nonprofits file the same Form 990?

No. Depending primarily on gross receipts and assets, an organization may file Form 990-N, Form 990-EZ, or the full Form 990. Certain organizations and activities have different rules. We confirm the likely form based on your facts.

Do nonprofits have to file separate state forms?

Often, but requirements vary. A state may require a nonprofit tax return, information return, exemption filing, charitable registration, or other annual report. We review the requirements that apply to your organization and its activities.

What is a state Attorney General filing?

Many states require charities that solicit contributions to register or renew with the state Attorney General, charities bureau, Secretary of State, or another regulator. The form name, attachments, exemptions, and deadlines vary by state.

When are nonprofit returns due?

Form 990 series returns are generally due on the 15th day of the fifth month after the fiscal year ends. State filing deadlines and extension rules vary and should be confirmed for each applicable jurisdiction.

Can you help if our bookkeeping is not ready?

Yes. We can assess the books and define any cleanup needed before preparing the returns.

Can you help with late or missing nonprofit returns?

Yes. We can review your filing history, identify missing federal and state forms, help organize the necessary records, and prepare prior-year filings. The consequences and available corrective steps depend on the number of missed years and the agencies involved.

Can Form 1023 be used to reinstate tax-exempt status?

For a 501(c)(3) automatically revoked after failing to file required annual returns or notices for three consecutive years, reinstatement generally requires a new exemption application using Form 1023 or Form 1023-EZ, as applicable, plus the IRS user fee. Retroactive reinstatement may be requested when the applicable requirements are met; approval is determined by the IRS.

Do you also provide ongoing accounting support?

Yes. Services can include QuickBooks setup and cleanup, monthly close, restricted-fund and grant tracking, financial statements, board reporting, and controller-level support.
